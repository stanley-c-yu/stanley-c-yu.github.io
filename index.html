About Me

My name is Stanley Yu, and I am presently a candidate for a Master's of Science degree in Data Science at Northeastern University in Boston, MA.

I first began studying Data Science in Fall 2017 when I enrolled in American University's M.S. Analytics program in Washington, DC. After studying for one year and working diligently to establish a strong foundation in programming with R and in Statistics, I enrolled in Northeastern University in 2018 to study how machine learning algorithms worked at a fundamental level.

Although the path to graduation since then has been fraught with many challenges, my time at Northeastern University has helped expand my capabilities in ways that I could have scarely imagined before 2017. Although I still have much to learn, I can say with confidence that I have become proficient in the use of many different tools and skills often used by Data Scientists.

As an up-and-coming Data Scientist, I have proficiency programming with Python, R, and SQL. In addition, I am also in the process of learning how to work with Java, and as I continue to gain more experience I aspire to learn how to work with other languages as well, such as C++ and whatever else I need to grow my capabilities as a Data Scientist.

Furthermore, I am familiar with a variety of packages commonly used by data scientists, including but not limited to the likes of NumPy, SciKitLearn, Pandas, Keras, ggplot2, & dplyr.

I am currently interested in opportunities to apply Data Science and machine learning to the fields of medicine and healthcare. Coming from a family of medical professionals and doctors, I believe one of Data Science's greatest contributions to the world is in its application of data analysis and machine learning to enable healthcare professionals to enhance their abilities to improve and save lives.
