About Me

Stanley Yu is a programmer and data scientist living in Boston, Massachusetts. He holds a Master of Science degree in Data Science from Northeastern University and a Bachelor of Arts degree in International Relations from Boston University. In addition, he studied Mandarin Chinese at the International Chinese Language Program at National Taiwan University in Taipei, Taiwan.

As a graduate student, he interned for the Human Rights Campaign in Washington, DC in their data analytics department. He also served as co-founder and former Vice President of Operations of the Data Science Hub at Northeastern University, a student organization dedicated to advocating the development of data science as an academic and professional field.

When not programming or learning new concepts in computer science, Stanley enjoys running, tennis, skiing, and long bicycle rides around the city of Boston. In addition, he enjoys reading science-fiction novels and books on history, as well as jazz music.

Select Projects

Community Detection of Twitter Hashtag Networks

  • Developed a network graph of Twitter hashtags related to COVID-19 that were segmented into communities based on co-occurence.
  • Contributed code to implement the Girvan-Newman and Clauset-Newman-Moore Algorithms for community detection, resulting in two network graphs with modularity scores of 0.8035 and 0.7892, respectively.


Classification of Chest X-rays Using Support Vector Machines and Random Forests

  • Developed machine learning models with a team of students to classify chest x-rays from the NIH Chest X-ray Dataset as having one of 14 possible medical conditions (e.g., Cardiomegaly, Effusion, Pneumothorax, etc.).
  • Implemented Support Vector Machine and Random Forest classifiers.
  • Collaborated with teammembers who implemented Logistic Regression and Convolutional Neural Network classifiers.


MNIST Neural Network Classifier

  • Implemented Feed Forward and Convolutional Neural Network Models using Keras to classify single-digit handwritten numbers.






Email Spam Classifiers
